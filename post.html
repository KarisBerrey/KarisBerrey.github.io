You Motivate You

Just a little something I was reminded of during my first semester of college.

Posted by Karis Berrey on January 12, 2026

To put it tritely, my first two months of college have been a very big adjustment. I have been learning some serious humility because I am now realizing my life isn’t as ironed and steamed as I previously thought.I will shoot straight with you. Many times over the last month, this transition to college has been really difficult for me, which is not what I expected, because I have always been that girl who loves changes and challenges. I did not quite expect myself to feel like I’m being pulled downstream by a strong current the entire months of September and October!In short, so far I have made a lot of mistakes. I have gotten hopelessly lost multiple times driving to work, struggled to turn assignments in on time because I’m a master procrastinator and will do literally anything else to distract myself from what I should be doing, and even accidentally fell down a flight of stairs and limped around campus for days (embarrassing...)!Over all, has my first semester of college been good so far? Yes.But has it been easy? Nope, not at all.Apparently, life is an endless series of learning curves, and college is noexception.In conclusion, I’m learning a lot from my first semester as a college freshman...so I thought I would share with you something I’ve been learning in college of late: You Motivate You. In college, you don’t have your mom or your teacher or your sister (or whomever) looking over your shoulder and suggesting that you do the next thing you need to do. No one is forcing you to do your homework. No one is requiring you to go run or lift weights on your own time. No one is making you go to bed and get enough sleep. No one is constantly reminding you to resist doomscrolling, to drink water, to eat healthily, to tithe on your paycheck, to read your Bible, to do your laundry, to pay your school bill...(I could go on!) It’s all on you, you, you, and you. And guess who’s responsible for the consequences of every choice you make and every action you do (or don’t) take? You. I’m not saying that I wasn’t personally responsible to do some of the things I mentioned above before college. But I didn’t feel the weight of these “to-dos” as much. Back then, perhaps I could blame my lack of discipline on the fact that I was on my parent’s schedule, not my own.Now, I can’t blame anyone but myself.I create my own schedule. I decide what classes I am going to take, how many hours I need to work and how active I will be in church, etc.In some ways, it is freeing to be able to make decisions like this. In other ways, it is straight up scary.Because if I do it wrong, if I don’t motivate myself to do what I need to do, if I fail...the responsibility for that failure lands solely upon my shoulders.Now all this is humanly speaking.I am a saved-by-grace Child of God, so ultimately Christ is continually sanctifying me (Philippians 1:6).As I read His Word and seek His Will, He will give me the strength to make decisions and pour grace on me when I fail to do the things I need to do or fail to be the person I need to be. That is such a comforting thought. Though it is intimidating to step into the realization that I am the result of my own choices and therefore completely responsible for my own successes and failures, I am also comforted, knowing that God promises to work all things together for the good of those who love Him (Romans 8:28).So yes, at college I do need to motivate myself, weigh my decisions carefully, follow through faithfully, and take responsibility for my own actions.But as I am seeking God’s face, He will continue to work on me through all of this. He is transforming me into the image of His Son, and as I make faithful, seemingly-insignificant-in-the-moment choices day after day, I need to keep this long term vision in mind.God is at work in me. He will strengthen me if I depend upon Him.So what should my heart attitude be as I journey deeper into this new phase of college, as I wrestle with responsibility, with myself, with my heart, and with my habits?The posture of my heart should kneel along the same lines as this incredible Scriptural prayer:You have said,“Seek my face.”My heart says to you,“Your face, Lord, do I seek.”Psalm 27:8 ESV
